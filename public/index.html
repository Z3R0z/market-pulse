Analysis

The AI trade is the market

Hyperscaler capital spending is the engine of this bull run. Google, Amazon, Microsoft and Meta plan roughly $725B in 2026 capex — up ~77% from last year — and AI-infrastructure beneficiaries account for about half of all S&P 500 earnings growth. Semis surged 80%+ in H1 before this week's profit-taking, and Meta jumped on plans to sell surplus compute as a cloud business.

Top 10 stocks ≈ 40% of S&P 500 market cap

Inflation flipped the Fed script

May inflation hit 4.2% — a three-year high — and markets are now pricing the risk of a rate hike, not cuts, in the back half. Fed Chair Kevin Warsh reiterated the 2% target this week without offering forward guidance. Thursday's early jobs report (moved up for July 4) is the next catalyst; ADP already showed private hiring slowing to 98K.

May CPI 4.2% · highest since 2023

Geopolitics & trade friction

The Iran conflict has disrupted flows through the Strait of Hormuz, and peace talks in Qatar faltered this week. Oil sits near $68 — but a sustained move above $100 would feed straight into inflation and rates. Add tariff escalation planned for late July and the non-renewal of the USMCA trade pact, and the policy backdrop is a live risk.

Hormuz tanker traffic ~4/wk vs ~102 normal (May)

Valuations leave little cushion

The index trades near 22× forward earnings, and the Shiller CAPE crossed 41 — its second-highest reading in over 140 years, exceeded only during the dot-com era. The bull case rests on earnings delivering: consensus sees ~23–25% profit growth in 2026. That's spectacular — and historically, growth that hot is hard to sustain, which caps how much investors will pay for it.

The read after today's close

Setup: constructive, momentum cooling at resistance. The daily technical composite still screens as a "strong buy" on moving averages (12 of 12 buy signals on MA5–MA200), and the index is consolidating just under the 7,600–7,621 ceiling after rallying ~18% off the late-March low of 6,344. Today's −0.22% dip was rotation, not breakdown: semis gave back gains while Meta (+8%) and defensives absorbed the flow.

The bull trigger: a daily close above 7,621 would clear the June record and, per chart watchers, open a measured path toward 8,000 — conveniently where the top of the fan chart below already sits. Bulls also get an earnings-season tailwind starting with banks July 14–15.

The bear tell: the RSI negative divergence plus moderate breadth means fewer stocks are doing more of the lifting. Lose the 50-DMA (~7,380–7,415) on a closing basis and the technical picture flips from "buy the dip" to "sell the rip," with the gap down toward the 200-DMA near 6,934 as the risk zone. Tomorrow's early jobs report is the immediate catalyst either way.
